The (No Longer) Filthy Bachelor

Pursuing a Life Approaching Functional Adulthood

Hi, my name is Scott, and I just turned 30. On the one hand, this was a totally arbitrary milestone. Physically, financially, emotionally - nothing in my life changed between the last day of my twenties and hte advent of this new, terrifying decade. But socially, 30 is an age fraught with significance. Depending on who you ask by this age I should have:
  • A steady career and some degree of financial stability
  • A romantic life that, if not already including a serious relationship, marriage or children, is at least heading in that direction.
  • Thoughtful opinions on serious adult topics like property tax and gas mileage.
  • And for that matter, a car or house, or at least an apartment rented from someone the average person wouldn't describe as "slum lord".
So while nothing material has changed, by merit of surviving long enough to start a third decade my filthy bachelor habits are no longer endearing. They're signs of childish dysfunction. So while I'm in no rush to chase after the above hallmarks of "adulthood", I did find myself pondering a question: what if I start acting my age?

The Issue At Hand

The filthy bachelor is a well-established archetype in contemporary America. Familiar cliches include: neck-based facial hair, refrigerators filled with take-out and empty mustard bottles, shower curtains hosting self-contained eco-systems.

At the same time that "grownup" America mocks these stereotypes, however, the average age of first marriage is getting higher and higher. We are rapidly becoming a nation of bachelors, so to me the question is: how do the unattached, unsupervised single men of America move into their 30s gracefully?

Cleaning Up Our Acts

I've divided my approach based on each room of the house.
Kitchen

Whoa Nelly! Wikimedia user Mysid

 The kitchen is one of the hallmarks of the filthy bachelor lifestyle - primarily its state of seemingly total disuse.
  • Buy some food
  • Clean dishes and fridge
  • Cooking easy meals like burritos and stir fry
Bathroom

 The crucible. Many seemingly with-it filthy bachelors are only revealed when a guest steps into their bathroom.
  • Clean the shower, sink, toilet and mirror.
  • Buy bathing products outside of Ivory soap and Axe shower gel.
  • Towels and toilet paper have specific functions and can't be replaced by paper towels and disposable napkins.
Bedroom/Livingroom

Dude, where's my everything?? Flickr: Ali West
  • Cut down on clutter with hampers, vertical storage space
  • Decor: Posters of action movies, videogames, and boobies deserve serious reconsideration.
  • Bed: wash sheets occassionaly. Make bed. Keep stuff tucked in.
Closet

Let's get you some shirts with buttons! Flicker: Peyri Herrera
  • Clothes that fit well are your best friend.
  • Keep hair clean and tidy (on head and face)
  • Be careful with hats and shoes - they are high-risk items.
The Front Yard

  • At thirty, you're expected to be peers with people who have families and homes. Just deal with it. Be polite.
  • Good posture and eye contact make you seem confident and in charge of your life.
